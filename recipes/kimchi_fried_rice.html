Kimchi Fried Rice

This recipe comes from Damn Delicous, who is an amazing chef that creates there own recipes that are stunning and mouth watering. I have only substitued a couple elements to suit our normal suppies available to us.

Ingredients

  • 1 and 1/2 cups cooked jasmine rice (best if a day or two old)
  • 1 cup Kimchi (coarsely chopped)
  • 2 tbsp kimchi juice
  • 3 tbsp coconut aminos
  • 1 tbsp gochujang
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 tbsp canola oil
  • 2 garlic cloves minced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 tsp fresh grated ginger
  • 4 oz shitake mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 cup matchstick carrots
  • 1/2 bunch kale, stems removed and leaves chopped
  • 4 fried eggs
  • 1/4 cup nori strips
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced
  • 1 tbsp black sesame seeds

Steps

  1. Cook the rice using your preferred method (we recommend a quality rice cooker)
  2. In a small bowl, mixed together the kimchi juice, soy sauce, gochujang, and sesame oil
  3. Heat oil in a large pan or wok over medium high heat. Add garlic, onion, and ginger, and cook, stirring frequently, until onions have become transulecent, about 2-3 minutes
  4. Stir in kimchi, mushrooms, and carrots. Cook, stirring constantly, until mushrooms are tender, about 3-4 minutes.
  5. Stir in cooked rice, kale, and kimchi juice mixture until the kale has wilted, about 2-3 minutes.
  6. In a seperate pan, heat 1/2 tbsp oil over medium heat. Fry 4 eggs until bottom is crispy and yolk is still runny.
  7. Serve immediately with eggs, nori strips, green onions and sesame seeds.
